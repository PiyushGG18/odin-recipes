Lasagna

Lasagna is a traditional Italian baked pasta dish made by layering flat pasta sheets with meat or vegetables, tomato sauce, béchamel or ricotta cheese, and mozzarella or Parmesan cheese. It is baked until the layers are cooked and the cheese is melted and golden brown.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna sheets (pasta)
  • Ground beef or Italian sausage (optional for vegetarian versions)
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Tomato paste (optional)
  • Olive oil
  • Ricotta cheese (or cottage cheese)
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Béchamel (white) sauce (optional, common in traditional Italian recipes)
  • Egg (often mixed with ricotta)
  • Italian seasoning (oregano, basil, thyme)
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Fresh parsley or basil (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Prepare the sauce by cooking onion and garlic, then adding ground meat (if using), tomato sauce, tomatoes, and seasonings.
  2. Prepare the chees mixture by combining ricotta chees with egg, Parmesan cheese, and herbs.
  3. Cook the lasagna sheets according to package instructions (skip if using oven ready sheets).
  4. Preheat the oven to 180 C (350 F)
  5. Assemble the lasagna by layering:
    • Sauce
    • Lasagna sheets
    • Ricotta mixture
    • Mozzarella cheese
    • Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used
  6. Top with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  7. Bake for 40-50 minutes, until the cheese is melted and golden brown.
  8. Let it rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing and serving.
